
# The Court of Dust

## Overview

The Court of Dust is the publicly-acknowledged authority primarily operating out of [[Bassuras]], composed of the leaders of gangs and crime syndicates. They control the flow of information in and out of the city and have their ears in other outposts throughout [[Marquet]]. No information is traded without their ears hearing it.

**Origin:** Post-Luxon merchants and thieves' syndicates in the ruins of [[Marquet]].

**Symbol:** A coin split in two — half buried, half visible.

Court rule weakens the further you get from Bassuras and does not extend to other major Marquesian cities. The Court is purely a product of the [[Hellcatch Valley|Hellcatch]]. Nearby settlements are independent but less developed.

## Goals & Methods

All the very best supplies are underground, in ancient buried temples, but the Court regulates entrances and exits. The general populace knows through whispers that it exists, but no one has ever seen it. Unauthorized excavations are prohibited and considered theft.

The Court responds to direct theft from Court properties and human trafficking with death — however the complicated and long legal process to prove a human trafficking claim means most accusations go unpunished, especially when the Court itself is the most common culprit. Wrenches are threatened with violence if they report unsafe or inhospitable conditions. The rules that apply to the people do not apply to the Court of Dust. Nevertheless, the citizens of Bassuras believe that if the Court were to vanish, the city would erupt into chaos.

The Court has spies and agents that are unmarked who live and work within the populace. Anyone in the city might be an agent of the Court, and that makes the threat of punishment everpresent.

The Court tolerates the myth of the [[Sanctash]] ([[Ashton Greymoore]]) because to do otherwise would enrage the city — and reshapes/alters the narrative instead, understanding the danger of letting the oppressed believe too strongly in a story about why they can and should rise up, while not being able to fully control the effect that story has on people.

As of the campaign's arrangement-phase plotline: the Court has already struck a deal with the Luxon mission house ([[Auris Vel]]) — political cover and eventual Krynn military support in exchange for sanctioned access to the underground ruins. The wider city does not know this deal exists.
