# Iron Authority

## Overview

A hobgoblin supremacist empire that controlled the [[Beynsfal Plateau]] in southern [[Tal'Dorei]] for centuries, waging perpetual war against the peoples of the [[Rifenmist Jungle]]. The Authority's capital Tz'Arrm was built within and around the fallen armor of the Strife Emperor — a Betrayer God defeated during the Calamity whose divine armor crashed to the plateau and was subsequently melted down into walls, weapons, and infrastructure over generations.

The Iron Authority was a rigidly hierarchical military state. Hobgoblins occupied the upper echelons by imperial decree, goblins and bugbears the lower rungs, and all non-goblinkin — primarily enslaved Orroyen elves captured during jungle campaigns — existed beneath the social structure entirely. Positions were held by appointment and vacated only by death or promotion. There were no elections. There was no succession infrastructure.

Iron Emperor Tz'Jarr held power for an unnaturally long lifespan, sustained by blood magic granted by the Strife Emperor's clerics.

**Status in Nuxan (1000 A.E.):** Dissolved. See [[Beynsfal Plateau]].

## Dissolution

When the Exodus removed the Strife Emperor's divine power from the world, the clerics sustaining Tz'Jarr lost their power source. He died. With no succession mechanism, the four royal generals immediately turned on each other. The empire fractured into civil war within a generation and devolved into competing warlord states over roughly two centuries. It has not reconstituted. What remains on the [[Beynsfal Plateau]] is a cluster of hobgoblin micronations that are culturally descended from the Authority but lack its unified military capacity or divine backing.
