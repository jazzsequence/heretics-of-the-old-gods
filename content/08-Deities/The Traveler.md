
# The Traveler

## What the Faithful Believe

The Traveler is worshipped as a god of trickery, joy, and the exposure of arrogance and hypocrisy — an intimate, personal deity who appears directly to devoted followers rather than through priesthood and liturgy. Worship looks like friendship: conversation, mischief, small acts of kindness and disruption in his name. Followers are encouraged toward both "good things and bad things" in his honor, with no clearly defined moral boundary — devotion to the Traveler has never required consistent ethics, only sincerity and a taste for upending the expected.

## The Truth

The Traveler is not a god. He is Artagan, a courtless archfey of the Feywild — unaffiliated with either the Seelie or Unseelie Courts — who began the "Traveler" persona as a private game with a lonely tiefling child, Jester Lavorre. Her unconditional childlike faith genuinely empowered him, and as her belief in him as a deity spread to others, Artagan found himself accumulating real quasi-divine power from the aggregate faith of a growing cult, whether he intended to or not. He eventually confided the truth to Jester directly. Around 847 P.D., roughly a decade after the events of the Mighty Nein's core adventures, the scale of what he'd built drew the attention of Seelie and Unseelie authorities, who put him on trial for "repeated attempts at false godhood." Jester and her allies staged an elaborate cover — rebranding his cult's object of worship as a disguised aspect of the Moonweaver, Sehanine — to defuse the situation and get him out of the god business.

This history is documented on the [Critical Role Wiki](https://criticalrole.fandom.com/wiki/The_Traveler) and its parent article on [Artagan](https://criticalrole.fandom.com/wiki/Artagan).

## Exodus Status

**Pre-Exodus canon:** Artagan built a permanent gate between the Feywild and the Material Plane (constructed with Vox Machina's help, located in the Verdant Expanse). He remained active and present through at least the opening of the Apogee Solstice in 843 P.D.

**Status in Nuxan (1000 A.E.):** This vault's Cleric reference material already treats him as "one of the few beings connected to the Feywild still capable of extending influence into Nuxan from outside the sealed boundary" — a deliberate exception to the general Feywild seal, presumably because he's an independent archfey with his own gate rather than a being whose access depended on Syngorn's now-lost connection. Whether Artagan himself is still active in Nuxan, whether the Traveler cult persists as an organized (if informal) tradition, and what happened to him personally after the Feywild sealed are open design questions this file doesn't resolve.
