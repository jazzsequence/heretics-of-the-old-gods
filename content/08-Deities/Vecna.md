
# Vecna

## What the Faithful Believe

Vecna, the Whispered One, is a Betrayer God and god of secrets, undeath, and hidden knowledge. Unlike the other Betrayer Gods who were Creator deities from the original pantheon, Vecna was a mortal archmage who became a lich during the Age of Arcanum, ascended to godhood in the time of [[Vox Machina]] (~810-812 PD), and was subsequently sealed behind the Divine Gate by those same heroes. His ascension was only the second time in Exandrian history that a mortal attained the power of a god.

Commandments: Learn all you can, and keep hidden that which you know. Reveal what pieces you must, but never the whole. Express and cultivate the evil within yourself, and in doing so, recognize it in others to exploit them for your own benefit. Seed the ruin of all who worship other deities, until only those who kneel before the Whispered One remain.

## Worship & Practices

Cultists calling themselves the **Remnants** are scattered secretive sects across Exandria. All Remnants remove their left eye (and some their left hand) as a symbol of devotion. They wear dark iron pendants with four prongs pointing upward over a single loop. Cultists give Vecna offerings of secrets and unwilling souls to gain his favor, learning the deepest secrets and keeping them hidden. They recognize evil as a virtue, cultivating it and encouraging others to express it.

The Remnants operate in extreme secrecy, rarely building alliances, wishing to remain an unexpected threat. Three known sects: the Unveiled Sight (seeks arcane lore in the Planes for Vecna's resurgence), the Devouring Blood (assassins using necromancy in the field), and an unnamed third sect.

## Perceived Powers

Dominion over secrets and undeath. Worshippers gain knowledge of hidden things and power over the undead. At his most powerful, Vecna's divine form could reshape reality — he was second only to Tharizdun and Predathos in power and threat level.

## Tensions with Rival Faiths

Vecna has an especial enmity with [[The Raven Queen]] (Matron of Ravens), as his command of undeath and his survival as a lich were direct violations of her domain over death and the dead. At the gods' final meeting before the Rites of Catatheosis in 843 PD, Vecna attended but stayed apart from the others, observing from a distance; when asked what would happen to him, he declared "every mystery could be treated as a problem to be solved" — implying he anticipated his own exclusion from the mortal rebirth cycle.

## Exodus Status

Vecna did not undergo the Rites of Catatheosis with the other gods. At the 843 PD meeting, Imogen Temult specifically advised against letting Vecna take on a mortal form so that Predathos could not consume him. His exact status at the point of the Exodus is canonically ambiguous — he was sealed behind the Divine Gate and had no mortal avatar, but is categorically not dead, not reborn, and not "gone" in any clean sense. He is a sealed Betrayer God who was never fully integrated into the mortal world and was explicitly carved out of the plan that the other gods followed. What this means for his situation 1000 years later is an open design question with real campaign implications.

The [[Whispered Rebellion]] — one of the eight factions in play — is specifically oriented around his potential return: some of its members aim to resurrect Vecna's fragment sealed in the Astral scar left by the Eldrazi war.
