# Soryn Vossira

## Description

Soryn was born in Rosohna to Lethren and Dernyth Vossira. Lethren was a librarian and archivist for the dynasty until he fell off a ladder and broke his leg, an injury that stayed with him the rest of his life — he became a stay-at-home parent through Soryn's childhood. Soryn's mother Dernyth was a soldier in the Krynn military, spending weeks and sometimes months away from home.

Growing up, Soryn was extremely socially awkward and introverted, more excited to get dirty in nature than socialize or play sports. They discovered a ruined, abandoned outpost outside the outer wards of Rosohna that they used as a hideout when they wanted to be alone — and were forbidden to return after Dernyth discovered them there as a teen.

Soryn went to a military academy to try to connect with and please their mother, despite their reclusive, passive nature. There they fell in love with another cadet who shared their intellect and out-of-the-box thinking. When the relationship was discovered by their superiors, their partner — a young girl named **Talyss Mirrenai** — was sent off to the Dwendalian Empire and never heard from again.

Soryn excelled at survivalism and outdoor drills, naturally suiting them to be an excellent advance scout, and rose through the ranks on unshakeable loyalty, honesty, and respect for authority. They've been sent into the field as Overseer of Light on a missionary tour largely because the Krynn military doesn't know what else to do with them.

## Personality

Wants connection desperately — largely what's kept them in the army, loyalty to comrades even when they don't fit in. Terrified of being left alone, even while naturally seeking solitary activities. Extremely introverted, but forms deep connections with the few people they truly trust. Their core conflict: they have a hard time finding and trusting connection, and haven't had many situations that taught them what a healthy relationship looks like.

When disconnected, they retreat into nature, longing for someone to share its wonders with. They learned their mother's directness and bluntness and struggle to lie even for politeness — which puts off or insults many people. Loyal and trusting to a fault, always willing to give someone the benefit of the doubt. This sits at odds with a military mindset built on just doing as you're told.
