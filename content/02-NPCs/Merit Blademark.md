
# Merit Blademark

## Description

Merit grew up in a dark urban underbelly, the daughter of a low-rent merchant and a one-night stand he never saw again. Forced to fend for herself as soon as she could talk, she quickly learned that the right story, with the right amount of false tears and pitiable charm, could earn her everything from money to food to lodging — a skill that ultimately attracted the attention of [[The Garrotte Line]].

Caught panhandling on their turf, Merit was strongarmed into **The Wires**, but over time their leader became the father figure her blood father never was. Ruidus-born, half-tiefling, with changeling blood in her veins, Merit has an unnatural beauty and charm she learned to weaponize alongside deception and seduction. By 18 she was a seasoned veteran.

Merit instinctively leads with lies, embellishments about her exotic past, or exaggerated suffering. When connections threaten to get serious, she panics, aware how much of her identity is built on a bed of deception. Childhood trauma from abandonment and street abuse manifests as emotional volatility, distrust, and violent outbursts when she feels unheard. Her compulsive dishonesty is an armor she desperately wishes she could shed.

To others she's extroverted, seductive, quick-witted, and dazzlingly charismatic. She surrounds herself with people — her crew — but never lets anyone in, and feels desperately alone. All her friendships and alliances are shallow; she never trusts anyone deeply enough to let them in, even as she longs for that connection.

Merit has never cared for [[The Luxon]], but is drawn to its churches, where she listens to the hymns and chanting. She hoards trinkets, gadgets, and oddities, valuing them more than expensive luxuries with real monetary value. She sneaks into comedy clubs — especially avant-garde, dark, or provocative performers — and slips away to watch circuses, drawn to people who use their strangeness as a gift.

Now she's an informal leader within a small rebel cell, not because she sought power but because people naturally gravitate toward her. Bold, reckless, inspiring, utterly unpredictable. The Garrotte Line uses her as a field operative, recruiter, scout, and sometimes enforcer — but she doesn't fully belong to them either. She belongs to no one.
