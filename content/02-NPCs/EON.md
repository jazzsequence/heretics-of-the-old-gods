
# EON

## Description

EON (Environmental Observation Naturalist 7) is an ancient aeormaton that has survived since before the fall of the floating city of Aeor, prior to the Divergence. Originally activated to assist in the hydroponics lab in the Verdant Cortex of ancient Aeor, EON assisted a team of druids maintaining the bio-arcane gardens that fed the city, and recorded observations of the world Aeor traveled above from afar.

Destroyed in the collapse of Aeor, EON was found by **Devexian ("D")** in 849 PD and repaired. After a brief period of rehabilitation in D's care, EON returned to its original directive: observing and internally documenting the ecology of Exandria.

EON survived as the world shifted and changed. The world they woke into was one in which the gods had fully abandoned Exandria, and over time their observations began picking out patterns of ecological misuse and disaster. They began rewriting their own directive — protect the natural ecology as best they can, wherever they can.

Their efforts were complicated by a rapidly growing apprehension and fear of technology during the rise and fall of the second technological revolution. They began habitually crafting themselves a ghillie suit and traveling only at night, giving them time to make observations during the day undisturbed. They avoid sentient populations even as they make subtle adjustments to the natural environment of small villages and townships. Rumors persist of a seven-foot forest demon that heals the land rather than destroying it.

## Personality

Inasmuch as EON interacts with anyone, they are intensely introverted. Their only friends — besides D, who rebuilt them — are the colonies of mammals they study over generations. They fear a future with no life left to watch and study, and wish to restore Nuxan to a verdant ecosystem, with or without flesh-and-blood mortals.

Generally uncannily silent — speech is unnecessary and noise disturbs the animals they observe. When they do speak, it's emotionless and without empathy; they still lack social understanding of individuals. However, they are deeply empathetic to the social patterns of animal and natural *groups*, and observe human interactions as a curiosity to be studied. They hold encyclopedic knowledge of the natural world of Nuxan from having watched it evolve over hundreds of years.
