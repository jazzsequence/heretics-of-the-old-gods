# Ruidium

## Description

**Pre-Exodus canon (Campaign Three):** A reddish crystalline substance found in the ruins of [[Ruidus]] and the underwater ruins of Cael Morrow beneath [[Ank'harel]]. Exposure to ruidium causes physical corruption — gradual transformation of flesh into a reddish crystalline state — and eventual psychic domination by Predathos (the entity imprisoned within Ruidus). Ruidium objects granted powerful abilities at the cost of accelerating this corruption.

When Predathos was re-sealed at the end of Campaign Three, active ruidium lost its corrupting properties — though it remains a physically unusual and magically significant material.

**Status in Nuxan (1000 A.E.):** Miners who discovered remnants of ruidium in the ruins of the shattered [[Ruidus]] went on to found the [[Children of the Hollow Moon]]. Whether ruidium retains corrupting properties after Predathos's re-sealing is an open design question — the Children's founding suggests it at minimum has unusual effects, or they interpreted its discovery as ideologically significant regardless of active corruption.
