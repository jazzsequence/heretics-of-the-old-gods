
# Lost Art Feats

*Homebrew feat category for Heretics of the Old Gods*

Lost Art feats are fragments of pre-collapse wizard curriculum — high-level techniques cut when the eight schools of magic consolidated into three. They aren't taught. A wizard reconstructs them from excavated theory, and doing so costs a feat. This is the "self-taught from Aeorian texts" path made mechanical.

## Rules

- **Category:** Lost Art. A Lost Art feat may be taken whenever you gain an Ability Score Improvement, in place of that ASI or another feat.
- **No ability score increase.** Each grants a full former subclass feature; that is the entire feat.
- **Prerequisites** gate on level and school. A former 10th-level feature requires level 8+; a former 14th-level feature requires level 12+.
- **School gate.** By default you can only take Lost Art feats from your own school. The GM may waive the school prerequisite when a specific technique is recovered as a story reward — an intact Aeorian text, a captured master's notes.

## Dunamancy

### Violent Attraction
*Prerequisite: Level 8+, School of Dunamancy*

When a creature you can see within 60 feet hits with a weapon attack, you can use your Reaction to add 1d10 damage of the weapon's type to the hit. Alternatively, when a creature within 60 feet takes falling damage, you can use your Reaction to add 2d10 to it. You can use this a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum once), regaining all uses on a long rest.

### Event Horizon
*Prerequisite: Level 12+, School of Dunamancy*

As an action, you emit a field of gravitational energy for up to 1 minute (concentration). Whenever a creature hostile to you starts its turn within 30 feet, it makes a Strength saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failure it takes 2d10 force damage and its speed drops to 0 until the start of its next turn; on a success it takes half, and each foot of movement costs 2 extra feet this turn. Once you use this, you can't again until you finish a long rest or expend a spell slot of 3rd level or higher on it.

## The Unseen

### Illusory Self
*Prerequisite: Level 8+, School of the Unseen*

When a creature hits you with an attack roll, you can use your Reaction to interpose an illusory duplicate; the attack automatically misses, then the illusion dissipates. Once you use this, you can't again until you finish a short or long rest, though you can restore the use by expending a level 2+ spell slot (no action required).

### Greater Portent
*Prerequisite: Level 12+, School of the Unseen (Portent feature)*

You roll three d20s for your Portent feature rather than two.

## Ruin

### Inured to Undeath
*Prerequisite: Level 8+, School of Ruin*

You have resistance to necrotic damage, and your hit point maximum can't be reduced.

### Command Undead
*Prerequisite: Level 12+, School of Ruin*

As an action, choose one undead you can see within 60 feet. It makes a Charisma saving throw against your wizard spell save DC. On a success, you can't target it with this again. On a failure, it becomes friendly to you and obeys your commands until you use this feature again. Undead with Intelligence 8+ have advantage on the save; if one with Intelligence 12+ fails, it can repeat the save at the end of every hour until it succeeds and breaks free.
