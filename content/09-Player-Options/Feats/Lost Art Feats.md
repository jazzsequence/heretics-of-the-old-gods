
# Lost Art Feats

*Homebrew feat category for Heretics of the Old Gods*

Lost Art feats are fragments of pre-collapse wizard curriculum — high-level techniques cut when the eight schools of magic consolidated into three. They aren't taught. A wizard reconstructs them from excavated theory, and doing so costs a feat. This is the "self-taught from Aeorian texts" path made mechanical.

Each surviving school has a single **Mastery** feat that reconstructs both of its lost high-level techniques at once.

## Rules

- **Category:** Lost Art. A Mastery feat may be taken whenever you gain an Ability Score Improvement, in place of that ASI or another feat.
- **No ability score increase.** A Mastery feat grants two former subclass features; that is the entire feat.
- **Prerequisites:** level 12+ and membership in the relevant school. The GM may waive the school prerequisite when the knowledge is recovered as a story reward — an intact Aeorian text, a captured master's notes.

---

## Dunamantic Mastery
*Prerequisite: Level 12+, [[School of Dunamancy]]*

You reconstruct the lost battlefield-gravity techniques of the pre-collapse Graviturgists. You gain both of the following:

**Violent Attraction.** When a creature you can see within 60 feet hits with a weapon attack, you can use your Reaction to add 1d10 damage of the weapon's type to the hit. Alternatively, when a creature within 60 feet takes falling damage, you can use your Reaction to add 2d10 to it. You can use this a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum once), regaining all uses on a long rest.

**Event Horizon.** As an action, you emit a field of gravitational energy for up to 1 minute (concentration). Whenever a creature hostile to you starts its turn within 30 feet, it makes a Strength saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failure it takes 2d10 force damage and its speed drops to 0 until the start of its next turn; on a success it takes half, and each foot of movement costs 2 extra feet this turn. Once you use this, you can't again until you finish a long rest or expend a spell slot of 3rd level or higher on it.

## Unseen Mastery
*Prerequisite: Level 12+, [[School of the Unseen]]*

You reconstruct the deep techniques of foresight and misdirection the merged school left behind. You gain both of the following:

**Illusory Self.** When a creature hits you with an attack roll, you can use your Reaction to interpose an illusory duplicate; the attack automatically misses, then the illusion dissipates. Once you use this, you can't again until you finish a short or long rest, though you can restore the use by expending a level 2+ spell slot (no action required).

**Greater Portent.** You roll three d20s for your Portent feature rather than two. *(Requires the Portent feature from the School of the Unseen.)*

---

## Ruinous Mastery
*Prerequisite: Level 12+, [[School of Ruin]]*

You reconstruct the necromantic minion-craft cut from the merged school. You gain both of the following:

**Inured to Undeath.** You have resistance to necrotic damage, and your hit point maximum can't be reduced.

**Command Undead.** As an action, choose one undead you can see within 60 feet. It makes a Charisma saving throw against your wizard spell save DC. On a success, you can't target it with this again. On a failure, it becomes friendly to you and obeys your commands until you use this feature again. Undead with Intelligence 8+ have advantage on the save; if one with Intelligence 12+ fails, it can repeat the save at the end of every hour until it succeeds and breaks free.
