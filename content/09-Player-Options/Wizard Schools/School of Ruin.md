
# School of Ruin

*Wizard Subclass — Heretics of the Old Gods homebrew, merged from the Evoker and Necromancer (PHB 2024)*

## Overview

Evocation and Necromancy were always the same trade under two names: the magic of ending things. In a world with an ungoverned [[Shadowfell]] full of accumulated dead, the blaster and the corpse-handler are the same feared, practical professional — muscle for whoever can afford them, a liability to everyone else. The merged school is a glass cannon that feeds on the deaths it causes. It does not raise armies; the minion-master's craft was cut in the collapse and survives only as [[Lost Art Feats|Lost Art]] fragments.

## Features

### Reaping Fire (Level 3)

**Grim Harvest.** Once per turn, when you kill one or more creatures with a spell of level 1+, you regain hit points equal to twice the spell's level, or three times its level if it's a necromancy spell. You gain nothing for killing constructs or undead.

**Potent Cantrip.** When you cast a cantrip at a creature and miss with the attack roll, or the target succeeds on its saving throw, the target still takes half the cantrip's damage (if any) but suffers no additional effect.

### Sculpt Spells (Level 6)

When you cast an evocation spell that affects other creatures you can see, you can choose a number of them equal to 1 + the spell's level. The chosen creatures automatically succeed on their saving throws against the spell, and take no damage if they would normally take half on a success.

### Empowered Evocation (Level 10)

Whenever you cast a wizard evocation spell, you can add your Intelligence modifier to one damage roll of that spell.

### Overchannel (Level 14)

When you cast a wizard spell of level 1–5 that deals damage, you can deal maximum damage with it. The first time between long rests is free. Each subsequent use before a long rest deals 2d12 necrotic damage to you per level of the spell, increasing by 1d12 for each use after that. This damage ignores resistance and immunity.

*Overchannel is the keystone of the merge: evocation output paid for in necrotic decay. It is the point where the two old schools stop being separate.*

## Note on the Lost Curriculum

The necromantic minion tradition was cut in the merge. **Inured to Undeath** (necrotic resistance) and **Command Undead** survive only as [[Lost Art Feats|Lost Art]] fragments — a Ruin wizard who wants to hold ground against the dead, rather than just outburn them, reconstructs these.
