
# Machinist

*Artificer Subclass — Campaign homebrew for Heretics of the Old Gods*

## Overview

Anyone with training can drive a crawler. A Machinist *is* the crawler — they built it, maintain it, and know every weld, every jury-rigged component, every noise it makes and what that noise means. In a valley where the Deathwish Run is a career and the racing syndicates and haulage gangs run on rigs held together by the people who understand them, a Machinist is the one who can coax a dying engine through one more run and knows exactly where to source the part that shouldn't exist.

This mastery is specific. A Machinist's power lives in their rig — see [[Rig Rules]] for the full vehicle system this subclass builds on. Separated from it, a Machinist is a capable mechanic and little more; their expertise is bound up in the machine.

## Features

### 3rd Level: Vehicle Expertise

You gain proficiency with land vehicles and with tinker's tools (if you already have tinker's tools, gain proficiency with smith's tools instead). Additionally, you can make **Resources checks** — an Intelligence (Investigation) check to source rig components, as described in [[Rig Rules]]. Anyone can attempt to source parts, but without this feature the Resources DCs are **5 higher**; you know the scrapyards, fences, and gang caches of the Hellcatch better than anyone.

### 3rd Level: The Rig

You have built a rig — a crawler-class vehicle, yours, that you maintain and keep running. Choose a frame (Skirmisher, Hauler, or Ruiner) and build it per [[Rig Rules]].

If your rig is destroyed, you can build a new one over downtime with tools, materials, and a fresh frame — though the specific accumulated modifications that made the old one *yours* are gone.

### 5th Level: Practiced Hands

Your familiarity with your own machine lets you do something no ordinary crew can: while you are at the helm, you can operate **one action station** — firing a mounted weapon, working a grapple rig, and so on — using your bonus action, piloting and fighting at once where anyone else would need a separate crew member.

### 5th Level: Field Repair

As an action while aboard or adjacent to your rig, you can perform an emergency repair drawn from your own kit. Choose one:
- If your rig has **wreck points**, clear **one wreck point**. (Wreck points must be fully cleared before a rig can be restored from 0 HP — see [[Rig Rules]].)
- Otherwise, restore **2d8 + your Intelligence modifier** hit points to the rig.

You can use Field Repair a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum once) per long rest.

### 9th Level: Overclock

As a bonus action while at the helm, you push your rig past its rated limits for 1 minute. Choose one benefit for the duration:
- **+10 ft** to the rig's base speed per segment.
- Advantage on the rig's control checks and mishap checks.
- The rig's next ram this turn deals maximum damage.

You can Overclock a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum once) per long rest. Each Overclock strains the machine: it takes wear damage equal to your frame's wear die × the number of times you've Overclocked this long rest (**Skirmisher 1d6 / Hauler 2d6 / Ruiner 3d6** per multiple). Your first Overclock of a long rest deals ×1, your second ×2, and so on.

### 15th Level: Indispensable

While you're aboard, your rig is never quite finished:
- **Field Repair becomes a bonus action.**
- When you use Field Repair to restore hit points, roll it **twice and use the higher total**.
- **Once per long rest**, you can perform an emergency restart: as an action, clear all wreck points and immediately restore your rig to **1 HP**, getting it running again. It must be properly repaired within 1 day or it immediately drops to 0 HP and regains any cleared wreck points.
