# WIZARD

Unauthorized arcane practice is illegal in [[Nuxan]]. The [[Cerberus Assembly]] and the [[Luxon]] church hold licensing authority over formal magical study, and the consequences for operating outside that licensing framework range from public censure to the kind of disappearance the [[Court of Dust]] handles through its East Dregs infrastructure.

A PC Wizard has one of three situations: licensed through official channels, which comes with obligations and surveillance; operating in secret, which is a daily survival calculation; or self-taught from excavated Aeorian texts, which means their understanding of magic is built from fragments, reverse engineering, and experiments that occasionally produce results nobody intended. All three are viable. None of them are safe.

Magic scarcity shapes which schools survive as living traditions. Some schools require institutional infrastructure that mostly stopped existing along with the gods. Others are deep enough, conceptually, that knowledge of them didn't survive the collapse intact. What remains are the schools where common practice, necessity, or specific institutional sponsorship kept the tradition alive.

## Available Schools

- **School of Divination** *(PHB 2024)* — Prophecy and prediction in a world that lost its gods' foresight along with everything else. A Diviner working for the right faction is worth more than most armed personnel, and the [[Court of Dust]] knows it.
- **School of Evocation** *(PHB 2024)* — Raw destructive output, carefully targeted. Evocation is one of the schools where shared knowledge and common practice survived the collapse most intact — an Evoker in Nuxan is either employed as muscle by someone with enough resources to protect them from the Assembly's attention, or they're very good at not being found.
- **School of Illusion** *(PHB 2024)* — Magic that hides by nature, which means it survives by nature in a world where unauthorized practice gets you disappeared. The [[Garrotte Line]] specifically values Illusionists for reasons the Line doesn't publicly discuss.
- **School of Necromancy** *(PHB 2024)* — In a world with an ungoverned [[Shadowfell]] full of accumulated dead, Necromancy isn't a fringe specialization — it's a school with constant, pressing relevance. A Necromancer is either a practical tool or a catastrophic liability depending on what they're reaching toward and why. The [[Echoes of the Raven]] and the [[Cult of Death]] both have opinions about Necromancers, and those opinions are not compatible with each other.
- **School of Bladesinging** *(PHB 2024)* — Elven arcane martial tradition, repurposed in Nuxan as the foot soldiers of institutional power. A Bladesinger is typically [[Cerberus Assembly]] muscle or a [[Krynn Dynasty]] asset operating under Assembly cover — magic integrated into combat discipline closely enough to read as physical training from a distance, which is exactly the cover the institutions sponsoring it want.
- **School of Chronurgy** *(Explorer's Guide to Wildemount)* — Dunamantic tradition originating with the [[Krynn Dynasty]]. A Chronurgy wizard in Nuxan has either [[Cerberus Assembly]] authorization or acquired this knowledge from sources that will make those ties complicated when they surface.
- **School of Graviturgy** *(Explorer's Guide to Wildemount)* — The gravity-manipulation school of dunamancy, same lineage and same implications as Chronurgy. The Assembly would very much like to know who's been teaching it outside of Dynasty-approved programs.
- **School of Blood Magic** *(Tal'Dorei Campaign Setting Reborn)* — Blood as fuel and sensor, the arcane tradition of the [[Claret Orders]]' wizard-adjacent practitioners. Blood is always available, which makes this one of the more resilient traditions to survive the collapse of formal institutions. This knowledge isn't widely taught, and coming into it means coming into contact with the Orders.
- **School of Occultism** *(The Crooked Moon)* — Forbidden knowledge from beyond the universe, accessed through a risk-escalating intrusion mechanic — the further you push, the more gets through from the other side. A hedge-witch tradition by nature rather than an institutional one. An Occultist in Nuxan is pulling at the same Far Realm-adjacent fabric that the Eldrazi war left scarred, and the effects that result from going too far are neither metaphorical nor controlled.
- **School of Biomancy** *(Heliana's Guide to Monster Hunting)* — Biological self-modification through surgical practice, with the wizard's body as the primary laboratory. A Biomancer's appearance announces what they are — scales, extra limbs, detachable hands, adhesive palms — which removes the option of operating quietly. In [[Bassuras]], where Wrenches already operate at the intersection of body and technology, a Biomancer is recognizable as a specific kind of person. That recognition runs in both directions.

## Excluded Schools

| School | Source | Reason |
|--------|--------|--------|
| School of Abjuration | PHB 2024 | Academic institutions of magic capable of teaching this school are largely controlled and rare |
| School of Transmutation | PHB 2024 | A conceptually deep school whose knowledge didn't survive institutional collapse intact |
| School of War Magic | Xanathar's Guide to Everything | There aren't war mages as a dedicated tradition in Nuxan; combat-integrated casting is covered by Eldritch Knight, Echo Knight, and Spellslinger |
| Order of Scribes | Tasha's Cauldron of Everything | Magic kept in books is dangerous by nature in a world where arcane texts are contraband |
| School of Philosophy | The Crooked Moon | Flavor doesn't fit the setting |
