
# FIGHTER

[[Bassuras]] produces fighters the way it produces everything else: through necessity, exploitation, and the shared understanding that if you can't protect what's yours, you won't have it long. Gang enforcers, Court-employed muscle, Blood Pit veterans, crawler drivers who learned that a gun handles a dust storm ambush better than a sword, ruins surveyors who made it back from the Hollows after everyone else didn't — Fighters here didn't come from academies. They came from situations.

## Available Subclasses

- **[[Battle Master]]** *(PHB 2024)* — Systematic, tactical, trained in maneuvers that don't exist in any formal curriculum because the curriculum doesn't survive contact with Bassuras's street-level reality. Battle Masters in [[Nuxan]] built their own systems from observation and repetition.
- **[[Champion]]** *(PHB 2024)* — Some people are simply hard to kill and harder to stop. The Champion isn't sophisticated. In a city that's been surviving on stubbornness for a thousand years, that's not a weakness.
- **[[Eldritch Knight]]** *(PHB 2024)* — Unauthorized arcane practice bound to combat discipline. The [[Cerberus Assembly]] doesn't sanction Eldritch Knights operating outside their supervision, which means an independent Eldritch Knight is either very careful or operating somewhere the Assembly doesn't look.
- **[[Psi Warrior]]** *(PHB 2024)* — Psionic ability in Nuxan is old, pre-divine, and doesn't require magical authorization. A Psi Warrior operates entirely outside the [[Luxon]]'s framework for what power is supposed to look like, which creates a specific kind of institutional anxiety in people who encounter them.
- **[[Cavalier]]** *(Xanathar's Guide to Everything)* — Mounted combat adapted for crawler culture. A Cavalier in Bassuras isn't on a horse — they're running point for a crew on the Flats, mounted on whatever can move fast enough to matter.
- **[[Echo Knight]]** *(Explorer's Guide to Wildemount)* — Dunamantic tradition originating with the [[Kryn Dynasty]]. An Echo Knight pulls a version of themselves from a timeline that didn't happen and weaponizes the gap between what was and what is. In Nuxan, a PC with this training either has Kryn Dynasty ties or acquired the knowledge from sources that will make those ties complicated.
- **[[Barrow Guard]]** *(The Crooked Moon)* — Tactics born from the grave — fighting discipline built around endurance past the point where endurance should be possible, and a particular comfort with death as an operational context rather than an outcome to avoid.
- **[[Gladiator]]** *(Apocalyptic Subclasses UA)* — The Blood Pits exist. The racing syndicates have informal bouts. The [[Court of Dust]] uses spectacle as population management. Gladiator discipline is theatrical, Charisma-driven combat — performance and survival fused into a single skill set, because in the arena they were never separate.
- **[[Starfaller]]** *(Scavenger)* — The Blood Pits also produce this, at the extreme end: someone shaped by enough concentrated violence that the world bends around them when they fight. The elemental storm effects aren't magical practice — they're what happens to a body that has absorbed that much for that long.
