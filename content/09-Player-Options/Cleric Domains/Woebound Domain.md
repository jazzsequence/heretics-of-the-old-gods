
# Woebound Domain

*Cleric Subclass — Heretics of the Old Gods homebrew*

## Overview

The gods chose mortality, and the world calls them gone. The Woebound refuses the word "gone." Somewhere in [[Nuxan]], their god walks as a mortal — footsore, nameless, deaf to prayer — and one day will remember what they were and return to lead the faithful. Until then the Woebound channels a divinity no one is present to receive, and the unanswered offering curdles: the life they give to others is torn out of them, and the decay they deal flows back as life. They are vampires of grief, keeping faith through a resurrection that has not yet come.

A Woebound chooses a deity like any cleric, but it changes nothing mechanical — the domain is a posture toward divine absence, identical whichever god is mourned. To the [[Luxon]] church this is heresy twice over: it insists a god still lives, and it names that god something other than the Luxon. To the folk traditions of the Hellcatch, who long ago stopped waiting, it is a sad and dangerous nostalgia. The Woebound wait anyway.

## Woebound Domain Spells

You always have the following spells prepared. They don't count against the number of spells you can prepare.

| Cleric Level | Spells |
|---|---|
| 3rd | Inflict Wounds, False Life, Gentle Repose, Wither and Bloom |
| 5th | Vampiric Touch, Speak with Dead |
| 7th | Blight, Death Ward |
| 9th | Enervation, Contagion |

## Grief's Ledger (Level 3)

Your magic runs on a vampiric exchange: the life it gives is torn from you, and the decay it deals feeds you. Your **grief die** is a d8.

- **Wither.** Once per turn, when you cast a spell that restores hit points, you take necrotic damage equal to your grief die. Necrotic damage you take from Grief's Ledger can't be reduced or prevented.
- **Bloom.** Once per turn, when you cast a spell that deals necrotic damage to a creature, you regain hit points equal to your grief die.

If the triggering spell fails to take effect — for example, it is countered — Grief's Ledger doesn't trigger, and no grief die is rolled.

## Channel Divinity: Grief's Shroud (Level 3)

As a Magic action, you expend one use of Channel Divinity and shroud a number of creatures equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum one) that you can see within 30 feet in mourning-decay — yourself included, if you choose. For 1 minute, the first time each turn a creature hits a shrouded creature with an attack, the attacker takes necrotic damage. This damage scales with your level in this class: **2d6** at 3rd level, **3d6** at 9th level, and **4d6** at 15th level.

## Directed Grief (Level 6)

You learn to control the exchange. When you cast a spell that triggers Grief's Ledger, choose one:

- **Redirect.** The grief die's result lands on a creature of your choice other than yourself, within 30 feet, instead of on you: that creature takes the necrotic damage (if your spell restored hit points) or regains the hit points (if your spell dealt necrotic damage). The triggering spell resolves normally.
- **Maximize.** The grief die's result falls on you as normal, and the triggering spell deals or restores its maximum possible value instead of rolling its dice.

## Grief Unbound (Level 17)

When Grief's Ledger triggers, choose one of the following. The level 6 options are no longer available to you.

- **Two targets.** The grief die's result is split onto two creatures of your choice other than yourself, within 30 feet — each takes the necrotic damage, or each regains the hit points, equal to one grief die. The triggering spell resolves normally.
- **Double.** Choose one creature of your choice other than yourself, within 30 feet, as the grief target. The grief die becomes 2d8. The triggering spell resolves normally.
- **Overflow.** You take the grief yourself. The triggering spell deals or restores its maximum value plus a roll of its dice (a 2d8 spell becomes 16 + 2d8), and the grief die's result on you becomes 3d8: you take 3d8 necrotic if the spell restored hit points, or regain 3d8 hit points if the spell dealt necrotic damage.
