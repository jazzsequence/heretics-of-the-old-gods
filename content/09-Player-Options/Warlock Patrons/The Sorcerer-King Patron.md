
# The Sorcerer-King Patron

*Warlock Subclass — Homebrew for Heretics of the Old Gods*

## Overview

This subclass uses the Sorcerer-King Patron rules as written in the **Unearthed Arcana 2025: Apocalyptic Subclasses** document. The features, mechanics, and class progression are unchanged. What follows are the patron-specific spell lists and descriptions for use in the Heretics of the Old Gods setting.

The Sorcerer-King Patron represents a warlock in direct service to a tyrannical, ancient, and overwhelmingly powerful entity — not a god, but something that has accumulated enough power, longevity, and influence over mortal life that the distinction has become largely academic. These patrons do not grant power out of generosity. They grant it because the warlock is useful, and they expect a return on that investment.

Four patron options are available. What they share: all four are ancient beyond mortal reckoning, all four have outlived the world they were born into, and all four have learned — in their own way, through their own damage — that power without purpose is just a longer way to suffer.

---

## Thesik

*Appropriate alignments: Neutral Evil, Chaotic Evil*

[[Thesik]] is the first among the [[Mind Lords of the Shattered Teeth]] — three ancient psionicists who transferred their consciousnesses into obsidian orbs millennia ago to preserve their power and their carefully maintained corner of the world. He was once a brilliant young man who loved pranks and camaraderie and the thrill of being the best at everything he attempted. That person is difficult to find now. What remains is a god of death who has been sitting in the dark for longer than most civilizations have existed, filled with regret, wanting nothing more than to finally end his existence, and unable to do so because the responsibility of maintaining what he built weighs too heavily on what is left of his conscience.

In the centuries since the Exodus, Thesik's depression has curdled into something more dangerous. He has stopped believing in preservation. He has started believing in acceleration. The world is dying — has been dying since before the gods left — and Thesik has concluded that the kindest thing he can do for it is to help it finish. He has made pacts with forces that would appall his younger self. He directs power toward ends that his oldest friend would not sanction and his lost love would not forgive.

A warlock serving Thesik is serving someone who has decided that destruction is mercy. Whether the warlock shares that belief, has rationalized it, or simply doesn't care about the ideology behind the power they've been granted is between them and whatever remains of their conscience.

## Barani

*Appropriate alignments: Lawful Good, Lawful Neutral, Lawful Evil*

[[Barani]] is the earth mother of the Mind Lords' trinity — or she was, once. She grew up on the shores of a sea that no longer exists in the same form, came to the Psionics Academy reluctantly, and fell in love with the most brilliant and most ultimately destructive person she had ever met. She has spent millennia in an obsidian orb paying for that choice.

Her power oscillates. The manic Barani is warm, generous, protective — a genuine earth mother who gives abundantly and cares deeply about the people under her protection. The depressed Barani is cold, irrational, and capable of genuine cruelty. She blames Thesik for everything that has happened to her, and she is not wrong to do so, and she cannot leave him, and she knows she cannot leave him, and this knowledge makes the depression worse. She has been cycling between these states for so long that neither is more real than the other. Both are Barani. Both are fully present.

A warlock serving Barani is serving someone whose alignment shifts. On good days, the power they receive is protective and restorative — the earth mother giving freely. On bad days, the same patron who healed their allies last week is cold and withholding and might redirect the warlock toward ends that serve Barani's anger rather than her better nature. The warlock never quite knows which Barani answered when they reached out. This is not a bug in the relationship. It is the relationship.

## Kosveret

*Appropriate alignments: Chaotic Neutral, Chaotic Good, Chaotic Evil*

[[Kosveret]] was once a quiet, homesick elf who found his first real friend in the person who pulled the most elaborate prank ever played on him. He was the god of joy. He laughed constantly. He was, by all accounts, the most fun person in any room he entered.

He is not that anymore. The millennia in the orb have taken whatever held him together and scattered it. He encompasses many personalities now, few of which are aware of one another. He spends much of his time wandering — psionically projecting himself across vast distances, impersonating people both real and legendary, following interesting things until they stop being interesting, then rewarding or destroying them depending on how they strike him in the moment. There is no predicting which version of Kosveret will answer a summons. There is no predicting what he will want. There is no predicting what he will find funny.

A warlock serving Kosveret is serving someone who might send them on a vital intelligence mission and then show up halfway through to ruin it because he got bored. Or might give them exactly the insight they needed at the exact right moment, for no discernible reason. The power is real. The guidance is not reliable. The warlock has chosen to serve a chaos engine wearing the face of a god of joy, and they should make peace with that before they start asking for things.

## J'mon Sa Ord

*Appropriate alignments: Lawful Neutral, True Neutral, Neutral Good, Neutral Evil*

[[J'mon Sa Ord]] — the Soul of the Jeweled City, the ancient brass dragon known in their true form as Devo'ssa — has ruled [[Ank'harel]] since its founding. They were once simply a very old dragon who loved conversation, collected information the way other dragons collect gold, and kept a city alive through patience and pragmatism and the occasional application of overwhelming force. They fought against [[Vecna]]. They watched the gods leave. They kept ruling.

A thousand years of being the only stable thing in an unstable world changes a person. J'mon stopped correcting people when they called them a god. Not because the title was accurate — J'mon knows perfectly well what they are — but because the people needed something to believe in, and J'mon was there, and the alternative was chaos. They built a templar system, granting fragments of their own draconic power to loyal servants who administered the city and carried J'mon's authority into places the dragon could not easily go. It is an elegant system. It is also, viewed from a certain angle, exactly what a Sorcerer-King does.

J'mon's interest in [[Bassuras]] is recent and specific. The pilgrimages to the drowned ruins beneath [[Ank'harel]] — [[Ur'Shakthu]]'s faithful seeking the Well — have not gone unnoticed. Something old and deliberate is directing traffic through J'mon's city toward something ancient and wrong below it, and J'mon wants to know what it is and why. A templar sent to Bassuras is an intelligence asset first and a warlock second. They carry J'mon's authority and J'mon's power and J'mon's very specific instructions about what to look for and what to report back.

The relationship is warm, by the standards of patron relationships. J'mon genuinely likes people. Brass dragons always have. They will ask the warlock about their day. They will remember the names of people the warlock mentioned in passing. They will also expect results, and the warmth has a floor beneath it made of something considerably harder than warmth.
